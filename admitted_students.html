Admitted Students: Obtaining a Form I-20

What is a SEVIS I-20 Form and why do I need one?

A SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System) I-20 is a United States government form issued by Mercy College for the purpose of certifying to the U.S. government that you are eligible for F-1 student status. The SEVIS l-20 form certifies that you have been admitted to Mercy College, that you are or expected to be a ‘bona fide’ student, that you will pursue a full course of study, and that you have enough funds to study and live in the U.S. You need this SEVIS l-20 form in order to obtain an F-1 student visa at the U.S. consulate or embassy in your country, and to maintain your student status while in the United States.

How do I obtain my visa?

The U.S. State Department is the government agency that issues or denies your visa. Its agencies are commonly known as Embassies and Consulates. Before traveling to the United States and in order to get an F1 visa, you must:

  1. Obtain the SEVIS l-20 form. This form is issued at the educational institution. You will be able to receive this form by mail from Mercy College once you have satisfied all requirements for eligibility.
  2. You must pay the SEVIS Fee prior to the visa interview at the U.S. Embassy in your country. In order to do so, you must have your I-20 Form to complete Form 901 (on-line) at https://www.fmjfee.com/i901fee/index.jsp and obtain the receipt or Form l-797. Please note that this process requires the use of a credit card. For more information regarding this topic please visit http://www.ice.gov/sevis/students/
  3. Complete Form DS-160 electronically at: http://travel.state.gov/visa/forms/forms_4230.html. This form requires to upload one (1) passport picture. Please make sure to carefully review the instructions prior starting the process.
  4. You must have a valid passport, letter of admission from the school, evidence of financial ability to meet expenses, and evidence of intent to depart the United States after completion of studies.
  5. You will be charged with a Machine Readable Visa fee and you will complete an ink-free finger print process during your F1 visa interview

Important: Students can apply for F-1 visas at any given time. However, the State Department will ONLY start to issue visas 120 days prior to the first day of the beginning of the academic program. In addition, international students will be able to enter the United States as early as 30 days prior to the beginning of the program.

For additional information regarding this topic, visit: http://travel.state.gov/visa/temp/types/types_1268.html and http://travel.state.gov/visa/temp/types/types_1270.html

Requirements for Issuance of I-20 Form

  • Full Acceptance into a Degree Program at Mercy College
  • I-20 Eligibility Form (to be completed by the student)
  • Copy of Current Passport
  • A Notarized Affidavit of Support (to be filled out by Sponsor and notarized)
  • Financial Documents in U.S. dollars – see I-20 Eligibility Form
  • First semester tuition and registration fee* deposit plus a one-time international student fee
  • Refund Certificate Form to be completed a party making tuition deposit If the student is denied for a visa, the tuition deposit will be refunded. A $100 processing fee will be deducted. A refund cannot be issued without this form.
  • F1 Transfer Form (to be completed ONLY if you are transferring institutions within the United States)

Please consult our Guide to Admission and I-20 Issuance for International Students for a PDF summary of the information provided on this page.

When should I submit all of the documents for my I-20?

Please note: The International Students Office cannot issue an I-20 form until all of the above requirements are met. All I-20’s must be issued at least 30 days prior to the starting date of each semester/term. If you are abroad, you must complete each and every requirement for I-20 issuance at least 30 days prior to the starting date of the term.

Where should I submit all of the documents for my I-20?

Please mail, email, or fax the above requirements for the I-20 form directly to the International Students Office:

Mercy College International Students Office 555 Broadway – Main Hall Dobbs Ferry, N.Y. 10522

Fax: 914-674-7455 Email: internationalprogram@mercy.edu

Immunization Forms

All students must submit the following immunization forms prior to their first day of class. These documents are not required for the I-20, but are required to attend classes.

  • MMR Immunization Record
  • Meningitis Immunization Form
