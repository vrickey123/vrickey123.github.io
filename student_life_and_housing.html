Student Life and Housing

Our Campus Locations

Mercy College has four campus locations each offering their own unique experience. The Dobbs Ferry campus is Mercy College’s main campus and offers students a traditional college experience with the opportunity to live in Residence Halls, eat in our Dining Hall, and use our athletic facilities. With views of the Hudson River, restaurant and shopping options on the Main Street of Dobbs Ferry, and a Metro North train station with service to New York City at the base of the campus; our Dobbs Ferry location offers students a safe and charming option with proximity to New York City.

Our Manhattan – New York City campus is located in the heart of midtown on 35th street, just across from Herald Square and Macy’s flagship store. There is always something to do and our students love having access to the world’s most amazing city at their fingertips! Our Manhattan campus is also home to our MBA program’s Strategic Consulting Institute and Computer Art and Design’s Roy Disney Center for Animation.

The Bronx campus is a brand new, state of the art facility and is home to the French Family Auditorium and the Marsha and James McCormick Library. Many of our Health and Natural Sciences students pursue internships in the nearby hospitals and medical offices. Mercy College offers a free shuttle bus from the Westchester Square – East Tremont Ave station of the 6 Train to the Bronx campus.

Our Yorktown Heights campus is home to 25 classrooms, 2 computer labs, and a library. The Yorktown Heights campus primarily serves the community of northern Westchester County. International students living in the area are welcome to enroll in classes there.

As a Mercy College student, you can take part in classes and events at all of our campuses regardless of which one you decide to attend. So if you’re attending Mercy College in Dobbs Ferry and have an internship in New York City, you’re can take classes at our Manhattan campus after work!

Housing

At Mercy, living on-campus is a safe, convenient and affordable option. On-campus housing is specifically designed to keep students connected with a computer lab, two wireless lounges with television, pool tables, kitchen facilities and a fitness room. They can also enjoy field trips, volunteer work, study sessions and movie and karaoke nights. Students can enjoy stunning views of New York’s Hudson River from Mercy’s Dobbs Ferry residence hall. Features of the offered accommodation include:

  • Fully furnished rooms inclusive of a bed, desk and closet
  • Single occupancy rooms available in addition to double, triple and quad occupancy
  • Cable, internet and phone connections in every room
  • Card-operated washers & dryers on the premises
  • Convenient parking for the students
  • Mercy’s Meal Plans at Hudson View Café
  • Central location for easy access to classes, library and athletic facilities

For more information on student housing and to submit a student housing application, please visit our Residence Life page.

Note: Housing is only available on our Dobbs Ferry campus. The Manhattan, Bronx, and Yorktown Heights campuses do not have housing options offered by the college.


Student Clubs and Activities

The Office of Student Activities was developed to ensure students have a variety of opportunities to be involved with the planning and facilitation of campus events. At Mercy College, it is believed that campus involvement is a necessary and integral portion of the college experience and students are strongly encouraged to participate in its numerous clubs, organizations, programs and activities.

Throughout the academic year, the Office of Student Activities coordinates a wide range of program activities and is committed to ensuring that every campus event is of the highest quality. In addition to providing on-campus activities, Mercy students can also take advantage of many of the numerous off-campus trips that Mercy offers.

Joining student organizations helps students develop and utilize various skills in leadership, communication, teamwork, and organization that they can carry with them throughout their academic and professional careers. It is also a great way to meet new people, have fun and make the most of their college experience. All clubs and organizations are open to everyone and starting new clubs are as easy as joining an existing one.


Athletics

NCAA Division II Athletics

The Mercy College Mavericks compete in the NCAA Division II East Coast Conference and train in top-of-the-line facilities overlooking New York’s Hudson River. Mercy offers intercollegiate, intramural, and recreational sports. All full-time students (at least 12 credits) in good academic standing are eligible to try out for college teams.

Mercy College's varied athletic facilities are available for use by both individuals and groups, and include:

  • Victory Gym
  • Mercy Gym (men's and women's basketball, and women's volleyball)
  • Baseball field (connected to Mavericks Field, an artificial turf field overlooking the Hudson River)
  • Smith Field (softball)
  • Outdoor basketball courts
  • Fitness center
  • Tennis courts
Intramural Sports

For students interested in non-competitive sports on-campus, Mercy College offers a number of Intramural Sports teams that are open to all students, regardless of your athletic ability. Please visit our Mercy Mavericks Intramurals page for more details.
