The Internatonal Application Process

Am I an International Student?

The International Students Office of Mercy College assists prospective international students with their application and student visa. If you are a dual citizen or permanent resident of the United States, please visit our Admissions page to complete your application. If you are studying at Mercy College through an exchange program at your home university, please visit our Center for Global Engagement. The information on this page is a guide to the F-1 international student application process and the F-1 student visa process.

Am I an Undergraduate, Undergraduate Transfer, Graduate, or Non-Matriculating/Visiting International Student?

You are considered an Undergraduate Student if your highest level of education is a high school diploma. You may have completed some college-level coursework in Advanced Placement or International Baccalaureate programs, or through college-level courses in high school.

You are considered a Undergraduate International Transfer Student if you have a high school diploma and have completed 12 or more credits at a foreign college or university.

You are considered an Undergraduate Domestic Transfer Student if you have a high school diploma and have completed 12 or more credits at an American college or university. This includes American community colleges and Associate’s degree programs. If you are currently studying in the United States and would like to study at Mercy College, please follow the application guidelines and submit the F-1 Transfer Form after you have been admitted to transfer your SEVIS Record to Mercy College.

You are considered a Graduate Student if you already have a four-year college or university degree (or U.S. equivalent) and are interested in a Master’s Degree program. If you completed your undergraduate program at a U.S. institution, please submit the F-1 Transfer Form after you have been admitted to Mercy College.

You are considered a Non-Matriculating/Visiting International Student if you wish to take classes at Mercy College without pursuing a degree. You may wish to transfer credits to your home university in your home country or simply study abroad on your year off.


How to Apply

The International Students Office encourages prospective F-1 international students to Apply Online. An online application is the fastest way to process your application and to receive an admissions decision. Please complete the undergraduate application, the graduate application, or the non-matriculating application as necessary.

If your prefer a paper application, you are welcome to complete the Undergraduate Paper Application or the Graduate Paper Application, and submit it to the Student Services Support Center address listed below, or email a scanned copy to internationalprogram@mercy.edu.

Mercy College operates on a rolling admissions schedule, which means that the Office of Admissions accepts and reviews applications throughout the year. However, the International Students Office requires that prospective F-1 international students submit all of their necessary documents for admission and their Form I-20 at least 30 days prior to the start date of their program. If you are unsure of your program’s start date, please view our Undergraduate and Graduate Academic Calendars.

Note: All application materials (application, credential evaluation, TOEFL/IELTS scores, letters of recommendation) from international students should be submitted directly to the Student Services Support Center at the following address:

Student Services Support Center Mercy College 2651 Strang Blvd Yorktown Heights, NY 10598-9910

Admissions Requirements

Good Academic Standing

Undergraduate

Mercy College requires that all applicants are in good academic standing at the time of their application. All students are encouraged to apply regardless of their GPA as Mercy College does consider other factors in the admissions decision such as extracurricular activities, professional experience, and English proficiency. Your application essay and letter of recommendation are also determining factors in your admissions decision.

Graduate

Graduate applications are reviewed by the program director and may have additional admission requirements. Prospective graduate students should consult their program’s website for information on specific admission requirements. For a complete list of our graduate degree programs, click here. Graduate applicants should contact Vincent Rickey, the International Admissions Recruiter, or the program director if they have any questions. Nevertheless, prospective graduate students must also be in good academic standing, and provide proof of English language proficiency and a foreign academic credential evaluation as outlined below.

Foreign Academic Credential Evaluation (FACE)

What is a Foreign Academic Credential Evaluation (FACE)?

A foreign academic credential evaluation is a document that equates foreign degrees to American degrees. Since Mercy College receives applications from around the world, we require our students to have their credentials evaluated for equivalency and authenticity. A credential evaluation considers your level of education and your foreign institution, and will provide an American equivalent of the courses you have taken, your final average, and your degree (for example, a French Licence to an American Bachelor’s Degree.) A credential evaluation ensures that your application will be reviewed fairly and under the same academic guidelines as your American peers.

Mercy College does not accept foreign transcripts or certificates, unless otherwise noted. This includes official foreign transcripts from foreign high schools or universities, certified translations, and certificates from examining boards such as the Caribbean Examinations Council or West African Examinations Council (WAEC). Official transcripts from high schools and/or colleges and universities outside of the United States must be evaluated by a NACES certified evaluation agency. Your evaluation can be sent by the evaluation agency to the Student Services Support Center at Mercy College or you can bring an official, sealed copy of your evaluation to an Admissions Office at any of our campuses.

Applicants who completed schooling in Puerto Rico and graduates of schools following an American curriculum, including schools in U.S. Territories, do not need to submit a foreign academic credential evaluation. Please contact Vincent Rickey, the International Student Recruiter, if you are unsure if your diploma is an American diploma.

Where can I get an evaluation?

You may purchase an evaluation from any NACES certified evaluation agency such as WES, Global Credential Evaluators, ECE, or SpanTran. However, our preferred provider is SpanTran. SpanTran offers a small discount and a fast turnaround time for Mercy College students, and our office works closely with their team. If you are already living in the New York City area, you can schedule an appointment with SpanTran to drop off your documents in person at their New York City office.

What is a Verification Pending evaluation?

If you brought your foreign credentials to an evaluations agency in person, your documents will have to be verified by the evaluations agency for authenticity. This usually means that the evaluations agency will call or email your home institution to verify you were really a student there. While Mercy College will accept unverified evaluations for an admissions decision, a verified evaluation must be sent to Mercy College by the middle of your first semester; otherwise you will not be able to register for the next semester's classes. In short, an unverified evaluation is treated in the same way an an unofficial transcript from an American institution.

It is the student's responsibility to contact their evaluation agency to send a verified evaluation to the Student Services Support Center (address listed at the bottom of this page) by the required date. If your credentials were evaluated by SpanTran, you can check on your verification status here: http://www.spantran.com/verification-status/

Undergraduate Application Essay and Letter of Recommendation

Undergraduate applicants must submit the following items with their application:

  1. Letter of Recommendation. You can use our Letter of Recommendation Form or you can submit a letter or recommendation from a teacher, counselor, coach, or professional reference.
  2. Application Essay. Essay topics and instructions are listed on our Application Essay Guide and below.

Application Essay Instructions

The essay demonstrates your ability to write clearly and concisely on a selected topic and helps you distinguish yourself in your own voice. What do you want the readers of your application to know about you apart from courses, grades, and test scores? Choose the option that best helps you answer that question and write an essay of no more than 650 words, and a minimum of 250 words. Please indicate your topic in the title of your essay. Choose only one essay topic.

For first-year students:
  • Some students have a background or story that is so central to their identity that they believe their application would be incomplete without it. If this sounds like you, please share your story.
  • Recount an incident or time when you experienced failure. How did it affect you, and what lessons did you learn?
  • Reflect on a time when you challenged a belief or idea. What prompted you to act? Would you make the same decision again?
  • Describe a place or environment where you are perfectly content. What do you do or experience there, and why is it meaningful to you?
  • Discuss an accomplishment or event, formal or informal, that marked your transition from childhood to adulthood within your culture, community or family.

For transfer students:
  • Please provide a statement of 250-650 words that address your reasons for transferring and the objectives that you hope to achieve.
